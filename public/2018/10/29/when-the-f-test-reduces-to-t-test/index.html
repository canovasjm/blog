When the F-test reduces to a t-test?

2018/10/29

If you have taken a regression or design of experiments class (or both), you probably have come across the following problem (or a similar one):

“Show that the sum-of-squares decomposition and F-statistic reduces to the usual equal-variance (pooled) two sample t-test in the case of \(a = 2\) treatments - with the realization that an \(F\) statistic with \(1\) (numerator) and \(k\) (denominator) degrees of freedom is equivalent to a \(t\) statistic with \(k\) degrees of freedom, viz, \(F_{1, k} = t_{k}^2\)”

Bye-bye words, hello formulas

Let’s start by putting all the wording in formulas:

We have to prove that

\[F_{a-1, N-a} = \frac{MST}{MSE} = \frac{\frac{SST}{a-1}}{\frac{SSE}{N-a}} \tag{1}\]

reduces to

\[t_{k}^2 = \frac{(\bar{y_{1.}} - \bar{y_{2.}})^2}{S_{p}^2(\frac{1}{n_{1}} + \frac{1}{n_{2}})} \tag{2}\]

When \(\color{red} {a = 2}\)

When \(a = 2\) the denominator of expression \((1)\) is:

\[\frac{SSE}{N-2} = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{n_1}{(y_{1j} - \bar{y}_{1.})^2} + \sum_{j=1}^{n_2}{(y_{2j} - \bar{y}_{2.})^2}}{N-2} \tag{3}\]

Recalling that in this case \(N = n_{1} + n_{2}\) and the formula for the sample variance estimator, \[S_{i}^2 = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{n_i}(y_{ij} - \bar{y_i.})^2}{n_{i} - 1}\] we can multiply and divide the terms in the numerator in \((3)\) by \((n_{i} - 1)\) and get:

\[SSE = \frac{(n_{1} - 1) S_{1}^2 + (n_{2} - 1) S_{2}^2}{n_{1} + n_{2} - 1} = S_{p}^2\]

\(S_{p}^2\) is called the pooled variance estimator.

When \(a = 2\) the numerator of expression \((1)\) is:

\[\frac{SST}{2-1} = SST\]

\[ \begin{eqnarray} SST & = & \sum_{1}^2 n_{i} (\bar{y_{i.}} - \bar{y_{..}})^2 \\ & = & n_{1} (\bar{y_{1.}} - \bar{y_{..}})^2 + n_{2} (\bar{y_{2.}} - \bar{y_{..}})^2 \\ \end{eqnarray} \]

\(\bar{y_{..}}\) is called the global mean and we are going to write it in a slightly different way. This is:

\[\bar{y_{..}} = \frac{n_{1} \bar{y_{1.}} + n_{2} \bar{y_{2.}}}{N}\]
